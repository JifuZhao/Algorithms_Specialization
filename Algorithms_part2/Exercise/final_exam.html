This was asserted in lecture without proof. To see why it's true, suppose (for the sake of contradiction) that G has two different MSTs T and T'. Let e be the min weight edge of G that is in exactly one of T or T'. Without loss of generality, assume e is in T. Adding e to T' creates a cycle C.     The correct answer is: A D D D D C A B


    Below are the sorted cyclic suffixes and the next[] array:

      i  suffixes[i]   t   next[i]
     -----------------------------
      0  A ? ? ? ? ? ? C     2
    * 1  A ? ? ? ? ? ? B     7
      2  B ? ? ? ? ? ? A     1
      3  C ? ? ? ? ? ? D     0
      4  D ? ? ? ? ? ? D     3
      5  D ? ? ? ? ? ? D     4
      6  D ? ? ? ? ? ? D     5
      7  D ? ? ? ? ? ? A     6

    Since first = 1, the first character is A (the first character in row 1).

    Since next[1] = 7, the second character is D (the first character in row 7).

    Since next[next[1]] = 6, the third character is D (the first character in row 6).

    Since next[next[next[1]]] = 5, the fourth character is D (the first character in row 5).
